# LLM Optimization

> Ensuring large language models accurately represent your brand and cite your content.

---

## What is LLM Optimization?

LLM Optimization ensures that large language models (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity) accurately represent your brand, content, and expertise — whether in training data, retrieval-augmented generation, or real-time search.

---

## Core Strategies

### Entity Consistency
- Exact same name, description, and attributes across every platform
- Maintain a canonical entity reference (About page, Organization schema)
- Inconsistent info reduces model confidence and citation likelihood

### Semantic Footprint
- Build content around topics and entities, not just keywords
- Create comprehensive topic clusters (hub-and-spoke)
- Cover topics from all angles: definitions, comparisons, tutorials, case studies, data, FAQs

### /llms.txt File
- Place at domain root
- Structured overview of site purpose and key topics
- Links to most important pages with descriptions
- Organization and expertise information

### Comparison Content
- Create "X vs. Y" comparison tables
- Cover your product/service category comparisons
- Balanced, factual data (not just promotional)

### Long-Tail Query Targeting
- Target queries of 15–23 words matching natural language patterns
- Align with how users interact with AI assistants
- Answer directly in first paragraph after heading

### Citation Readiness
- Lists and tables for clean AI extraction
- Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences = one citable idea)
- Original data and proprietary statistics
- Lead-with-answer format
- Definitive statements ("X is Y" not "X might be Y")

## AI Bot Governance

### Block Training Bots
- GPTBot (OpenAI training)
- ClaudeBot (Anthropic training)
- Google-Extended (AI training)
- CCBot (Common Crawl)
- Bytespider (ByteDance)

### Allow Citation Bots
- OAI-SearchBot (OpenAI search)
- ChatGPT-User (live browsing)
- PerplexityBot (search citations)
- Applebot-Extended (Siri/AI)
- Googlebot, Bingbot (search indexing)
